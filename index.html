About Me

I'm a second-year undergraduate at Imperial College London, currently pursuing a Master of Engineering in Aeronautical Engineering. Outside of my interest in all things flying, I'm trying, exploring and learning many new things!

Currently, I'm most interested in machine learning applications, mainly in reinforcement learning. I'm also interested in the application and simulation of decision theory, and computational methods used in fluid dynamics and other possible engineering applications.

This site is my attempt to catalogue everything I do as I try to deepen my understanding, increase my proficiency, and follow up on what I am doing. Please cheer me on as I try my best!

cool stuff I'm working on!

Double-dummy solver

Currently still under work. A double-dummy solver for contract bridge that will output optimal contracts based on suit and final optimal contract based on board, from an input of .lin file, utilising minimax algorithm with alpha-beta pruning.

Planeworld

An attempt in reinforcement learning to simulate how planes take-off and land in an airstrip in order to understand and optimise how such processes are managed currently.

Games

Environments written in Python for different games in order to test out different decision-making algorithms alongside reinforcement learning solutions.
